Title Excerpt
Analytic Summary & Response - People of the Screen Like many born in the late 90s, I had grown up reading books before they started to phase out by being replaced by computers. Some argue this was for the worst as an article published in The New Atlantis titled “People of the Screen” by the editor and author Christine Rosen in the fall of 2008. Rosen insists that the age of the book is coming to an end as screen readers continue to grow in numbers and book readers decline further.
The Greatest Country in the World is Lagging Behind - Political Science Analysis Paper It’s no new topic to behold, the United States of America has been lagging behind for some time in the field of healthcare. Within our own borders the topic is filled with heated debates and outside our system in place is often the butt of a joke. But like all other countries on the Earth that utilize a form of Healthcare, the U.S must follow a specific model or set of guidelines, right?
The Obsessive Binds - Sherry Turkle Response The advancement of technology has allowed people not only to connect instantaneously but also allowed them to disconnect from the real world to destress. Yet as it progresses, the benefits have found ways to reverse the positive effects and create addictive and antisocial behaviors in compulsive users. Sherry Turkle, a published author with a Ph.D. in Sociology and Personality Psychology BA in Social Studies, is concerned over the growing reliance on technology people have developed.
The Blue Truth - The Effects of Blue Light Avid cell phone users may know the feeling of being jolted awake by the bright flash and small buzz of their cellular device. Those that have experience with such an event would understand checking it would lead to a restless night as they continue to browse after being woken up, hoping to fall asleep eventually. Like those who have gone through the motions before already know, it seldom ends that way.
